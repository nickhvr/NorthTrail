A good day-hike setup is simple: carry enough water, weather protection, navigation, food and a few emergency basics. Most beginners overpack the wrong things and forget the important ones.

## Build one standard setup

Start with a comfortable daypack, then add water, snacks, a light rain jacket, a power bank, a small first-aid kit and a basic insulating layer. If the weather can change, pack for the worst realistic case, not the current parking-lot conditions.

## The mistake most beginners make

The biggest error is not building a repeatable system. A fixed checklist saves money, reduces decision fatigue and keeps every future hike easier to prepare.
