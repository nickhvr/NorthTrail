For most beginners, comfort beats technical specs. A boot that fits well and matches the terrain is worth more than a heavy, overbuilt model marketed for extreme use.

## Start with actual terrain

Forest trails, gravel paths and occasional hills need a very different boot than alpine trekking. Weight, grip and fit should guide the decision before you think about premium features.

## Waterproof or not?

Waterproofing helps in wet grass and mixed weather, but it can also reduce breathability. For many beginners, the better choice is a comfortable, lighter boot or shoe matched to the season.
